Dance, Anatomy, and Social Media

An exploration of how dance-focused Instagrams do — or do not — promote healthy dance practices as understood through anatomy.

Social Media & Dance

Regardless of your opinions on the rise of social media in the 21st century, it’s near impossible to argue against the reality that social media platforms have had a profound influence on the ways that people share and consume information. In many ways, this has significant benefits to dancers in training – the social media accounts of professional dancers as well as “celebrity” students can provide inspiration and motivation by offering a glimpse into their day-to-day lives.

However, social media posts tend to be carefully crafted to portray certain themes and send implicit messages. Instagram, in particular, is often accused of promoting an excessively “image-focused” online presence.

There are many popular dance accounts on Instagram, spanning across different styles, levels of experience, and geographic locations. While dance accounts on Instagram can offer inspiration and meaningful advice, they can also implicitly encourage unsafe practices for dance training.

In this exploration, we will focus on three topics: extended static stretching, hip impingement (related to training for extensions), and foot stretching. Since these are all common topics on Instagram, we will only consider a small sampling of posts related to these areas.

Long Static Stretching

If you're an Instagram user, you've probably seen the pictures — the professional dancer sprawled in oversplit, casually messing with their phone or eating a snack. Despite this popular image, extended static stretching is not an effective practice for improving functional flexibility.

Training for Hip Extension

Sky-high extensions are a popular aesthetic in dance right now. Tips for achieving these extensions are certainly a common topic on Instagram; however, some suggestions are potentially harmful for unprepared dancers, leaving them vulnerable to hip impingement and other pitfalls.

Foot Stretching

Another quasi-obsession in dance (particularly in ballet) is the highly arched, excessively flexible foot. Popular dance accounts often post about foot-stretching — the practice of using various devices to increase static foot flexibility.
